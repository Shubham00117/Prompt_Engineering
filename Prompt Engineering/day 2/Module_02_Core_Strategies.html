8Ô∏è‚É£-9Ô∏è‚É£ Chain-of-Thought (CoT) & Reasoning

Core Idea: Forcing the model to "show its work" before giving the final answer. This dramatically improves accuracy in math and logic. 
PROMPT: "Solve X. Think step-by-step."
   ‚Üì
THOUGHT 1: [Identify variables]
THOUGHT 2: [Apply formula]
THOUGHT 3: [Verify units]
   ‚Üì
FINAL ANSWER: [Precise result]
Trigger Phrases:
‚Ä¢ "Let's think step by step."
‚Ä¢ "Walk through your reasoning process."
‚Ä¢ "Explain your math before the final result."
üí° Least-to-Most Prompting
For very hard logic: Break the problem into sub-problems. Ask the AI to solve the simplest part first, then use that answer to solve the next part. This "progressive solving" reduces errors in complex coding tasks.

üîü-1Ô∏è‚É£1Ô∏è‚É£ Self-Verification & Error-Checking

Core Idea: Build a two-stage process where the model first generates, then audits its own output.

1Ô∏è‚É£2Ô∏è‚É£-1Ô∏è‚É£3Ô∏è‚É£ Effective Example Crafting

Core Idea: Not all examples are equal. Good examples are diverse, clear, and relevant.
‚úÖ Positive vs Negative Pattern:
Show the AI one GOOD example and one BAD example. Explain WHY the bad one failed to help the AI avoid similar mistakes.
Principles of Good Examples:
1. Representative: Cover typical real-world cases.
2. Diverse: Don't use 3 examples that are all 90% identical.
3. Clear Labeling: Use "Input:" and "Output:" markers.
